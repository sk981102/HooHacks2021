Does it matter which Covid-19 Vaccine we take?


The health care providers have mentioned that "No, any vaccine is good vaccine."

Some may be concerned that Johnson and Johnson Covid-19 vaccine has lower effective rate thus ineffective. However, those clinical trials were done during different time period; Johnson and Johnson has conducted its trial in different countries as opposed to Moderna and Pfizer, which conducted their studies only in the U.S.

Among all three vaccine manufacturers, they all reported that no patients who got Covid-19 after the vaccination were in severe conditions. Thus, all of them are working.

{% endif %} {% if form.second_shot %}

Why does second shot might cause greater side effects?


In fact, more people report that they feel ill after getting their second dose. The common side effects include such as fever, fatigue, headache, and soreness at the injection site on their arm. However, this is a good sign that your body's immune system is fighting the new strand of virus. Also, most side effects go away within 72 hours and maximum 7 days. So do not worry!
{% endif %} {% if form.side_effect %}

What are the major side effects of Covid-19 vaccine?


In the arm where you got the shot, the common side effects include: Pain, Redness, Swelling.
Throughout the rest of your body,the common side effects include: Tiredness, Headache, Muscle pain, Chills, Fever,Nausea.

Can my children take the Covid-19 Vaccine?


There has not been enough clinical trial conducted by the vaccine manufacturers to determine its effect. However, Moderna and Pfizer are in the process of their clinical trial on younger teenagers. We will have more updates coming soon.
